June 6, 1944 — Operation Overlord

Overview

D-Day, known as Operation Overlord, was the largest seaborne invasion in history. On June 6, 1944, Allied forces stormed the beaches of Normandy, France, marking the beginning of the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi control.

Public domain (U.S. Army / Wikimedia Commons)

Planning the Invasion

The Allies spent months planning Operation Overlord, involving deception strategies such as Operation Fortitude to mislead the Germans about the landing site. Supreme Allied Commander Dwight D. Eisenhower oversaw the coordination of forces from the U.S., Britain, Canada, and other nations.

The Battle

On June 6, 1944, over 156,000 troops landed on five beachheads: Utah, Omaha, Gold, Juno, and Sword. Despite heavy resistance, particularly at Omaha Beach, the Allies established a crucial foothold in France.

Aftermath

The success of D-Day allowed the Allies to push deeper into France, leading to the liberation of Paris in August 1944. It marked a turning point in the war in Europe and demonstrated the effectiveness of Allied cooperation.
